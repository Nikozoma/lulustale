DEMO SCOPE RULES — v2.1 FOUNDATION RESTART

Build the proof-of-concept, not the full game.

Current implementation scope:
Build the indoor home foundation only until the user manually confirms it works well on phone.

Required for the home foundation:
- Mobile-first landscape PWA.
- Top-down web-based 2D game.
- Responsive virtual viewport.
- Player-centered camera.
- Dual-zone mobile controls.
- Tap-to-interact.
- Dialogue boxes with Next-button-only progression.
- Proximity-gated interactions.
- Home semantic map import.
- Real Lulu/player sprite and directional animation.
- Real home/interior/furniture assets from project asset packs.
- Real dog asset.
- Bed, fridge, dog bowl/dog interaction, exit door, room collision/walkable layout.
- Tiny Day 1 home tutorial quest chain.

Required home tutorial loop:
1. Lulu starts near/at bed.
2. Player learns movement.
3. Objective sends Lulu to fridge.
4. Player interacts with fridge.
5. Lulu obtains dog food.
6. Objective sends Lulu to dog/dog bowl.
7. Player feeds dog / uses dog bowl.
8. Dog reacts.
9. Objective points to exit door, but exterior transition may remain disabled/deferred until the foundation is approved.

Strict no-placeholder rule:
- Do not use colored boxes/shapes as runtime replacements for Lulu, dog, bed, fridge, doors, home tiles, core furniture, interactables, or map layout.
- Do not improvise rooms, objects, or stairs not in the semantic map.
- Do not use fake home geometry.
- If a required real asset or map object cannot be located/sliced/implemented, stop and report the exact blocker.

Allowed temporary debug aids:
- Debug overlays, coordinate labels, collision outlines, and dev-only markers are allowed only if clearly marked as debug and not used as shipped/core art.
- Semantic colors in the Tilemap Builder are allowed because they are layout data, not final runtime art.

Do not build yet unless explicitly requested:
- Full overworld.
- Charles Jr. map/interior.
- Bird theft/chase/fries recovery.
- Pass 2 night content.
- Full open-world neighborhood.
- Fred Meyer or 7-Eleven content.
- Real-time or automatically advancing 24-hour clock.
- Day/night timers or fixed phase-length settings.
- Full NPC schedules.
- Full shop economy.
- Full inventory UI.
- Full save/load system.
- Full insanity power tree.
- Cars/traffic system.
- Complex stealth.
- Complex combat.
- Final art/audio.
- Android APK wrapper.

Design principle:
Every feature in the foundation should either prove asset loading/scaling, prove the home layout/collision, teach a core interaction, or establish the tone of Lulu's Tale.

Time-system rule:
Day and night are manual phases controlled from Lulu's bed. Daytime bed use offers "sleep until next day" or "stay up into night"; nighttime bed use advances to the next day. Exploration is never placed on a clock.
