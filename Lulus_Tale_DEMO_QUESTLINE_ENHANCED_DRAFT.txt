LULU'S TALE — ENHANCED DEMO QUESTLINE DRAFT
============================================

Document status
---------------

Creative design pass for review.
This is not final dialogue and is not an implementation file.
It strengthens the existing demo quest without replacing its core story.

Working title
-------------

FRIES, FEATHERS, AND POOR DECISIONS

One-line premise
----------------

Lulu leaves home for fries, gets dragged into a territorial dispute with the
neighborhood birds, and discovers that the same parking lot follows different
rules after dark.

CORE MATERIAL PRESERVED FROM THE ORIGINAL
=========================================

The revised quest still contains the original defining sequence:

1. Start at Home.
2. Check the fridge.
3. Feed/check on the dog.
4. Leave Home and cross the Overworld.
5. Enter Charles Jr.
6. Get fries.
7. A bird steals or attempts to steal the fries.
8. Find the bird and recover/investigate the fries.
9. Return home.
10. Use the bed.
11. Enter night through the player's bed choice.
12. Go outside at night.
13. Discover the bird gang threat.
14. Find a sword/weapon in a bush.
15. Defeat three birds.
16. Return home.
17. Go back to bed.
18. End the demo with a Day 2 teaser.

What this pass improves
-----------------------

- Gives the dog/fridge opening a clearer cause-and-effect chain.
- Lets the bird incident branch without allowing the story to break.
- Makes the recovered fries outcome believable rather than automatically edible.
- Gives the player a clue that motivates staying up.
- Respects the manual bed-based day/night design.
- Introduces the night guide before the bird gang battle.
- Uses the existing real Overworld spaces more intentionally.
- Converts the bird fight into a turn-based tutorial encounter.
- Makes the birds recurring comic antagonists rather than disposable enemies.
- Provides exact quest stages, flags, items, events, and convergence rules for a
  future data-driven quest implementation.

TARGET EXPERIENCE
=================

Tone
----

- Warm everyday slice-of-life during the day.
- Increasingly specific bird-related absurdity.
- Quiet, surreal neighborhood atmosphere at night.
- Comedy comes from characters treating nonsense with different levels of
  seriousness.
- The night is strange, not immediately apocalyptic.

Estimated first-play length
---------------------------

- Day route and exploration: roughly 15-25 minutes.
- Night route and first battle: roughly 15-25 minutes.
- Total target: approximately 30-50 minutes depending on exploration, reading,
  and battle pace.

Player promises
---------------

- No day/night timer.
- No permanently missed night quest for choosing sleep.
- No hard quest failure from the fry-defense minigame.
- No random battle before the authored bird-gang encounter.
- Clear objective text and one active "!" marker at a time.
- A safe retry if the tutorial battle is lost.

QUEST STRUCTURE
===============

Use two linked quests rather than one enormous quest state machine.

Quest A
-------

ID: quest.demo.fries_and_feathers_day
Title: Fries and Feathers
Role: Main demo quest, daytime half

Quest B
-------

ID: quest.demo.birds_after_dark
Title: Birds After Dark
Role: Main demo quest, night half

Why split them
--------------

- The player's bed choice is a clean boundary.
- Quest A can complete whether the player sleeps or stays up.
- Quest B can be unlocked but remain pending until any night phase.
- Choosing normal sleep does not force, fail, or erase night content.
- Save/load and journal presentation are easier to understand.

ACT 1 — HOME: THE IMPORTANT BREAKFAST
=====================================

Purpose
-------

Introduce Lulu, touch movement, the objective marker, Home, the fridge, the dog,
and the game's comedy without a long exposition scene.

Opening state
-------------

- Map: Home
- Phase: Day
- Day: 1
- Lulu starts at the current Home player spawn.
- A short movement tutorial appears only on a first save.
- First active objective: "Check the fridge."

Stage A01 — Check the fridge
----------------------------

ID: check_fridge
Objective: Check the fridge.
Target: Home / fridge_interaction

Interaction result:

- Lulu finds dog food.
- The human-food situation is technically not empty, but emotionally empty.
- Give item.dog_food x1.
- Set flag.demo.checked_fridge = true.

Suggested dialogue beat:

  LULU: Dog food, mustard, and an ice cube with seniority.
  LULU: Breakfast has made its position clear.

The dog reacts from the next room, establishing the next objective without an
arbitrary task assignment.

Stage A02 — Feed the dog
------------------------

ID: feed_dog
Objective: Feed the dog.
Target: Home / dog_interaction
Required item: item.dog_food x1

Interaction result:

- Remove item.dog_food x1.
- Set flag.demo.dog_fed = true.
- The dog's enthusiasm makes Lulu more aware that she has not eaten.

Suggested dialogue beat:

  LULU: One of us is having breakfast.
  DOG: [the sound of a bowl becoming somebody else's problem]
  LULU: Great. Now I want fries.

Stage A03 — Leave for Charles Jr.
--------------------------------

ID: leave_home_for_charles
Objective: Go to Charles Jr.
Target sequence:

1. Home / entrance_exit
2. Overworld / transition_to_charles_jr

On Home exit:

- Transition to the current Overworld Home spawn.
- Keep the same objective text.
- The Charles Jr. transition becomes the only active marker.

Optional ambient interactions
-----------------------------

These add flavor but never create marker spam or block progress:

- Re-check fridge: "Still dog food. Still not a fry."
- Re-check dog after feeding: dog is already asleep or intensely satisfied.
- Apartment sign: establishes the building as Lulu's home landmark.

On transition to Overworld:

ACT 4 — THE FRY INCIDENT
========================

Purpose
-------

Introduce the recurring fry thief, a mobile minigame, meaningful outcomes, and
the first hint that the birds are organized.

Location
--------

Accessible parking/walkway immediately south of the Charles Jr. transition.
Suggested semantic trigger: bird_fry_ambush_trigger near tile (14,21), subject to
final Combined View confirmation.

Event A06 — Ambush setup
------------------------

ID: event.demo.bird_fry_ambush

Sequence:

1. Lock world input.
2. A bird lands ahead of Lulu and looks directly at the fry bag.
3. Lulu notices the look.
4. The bird lunges.
5. Start minigame.demo.defend_fries.

Suggested dialogue beat:

  LULU: No.
  BIRD: ...
  LULU: I know that look.

Fry-defense minigame
--------------------

Working name: Secure the Bag

Mechanic direction:

- A large touch target tracks the bird's lunge direction or timing.
- Several short reaction beats fill one defense meter.
- It lasts only a few seconds; this is an encounter timer, not a world timer.
- Return success, partial, or fail.

Outcome matrix
--------------

SUCCESS — "Mostly secured"

- Lulu saves most of the fries.
- The bird escapes with one fry because it refuses to leave empty-beaked.
- Keep item.charles_fries, but replace its usable state with
  item.emergency_fry_portion x1 for the later battle.
- Set variable.demo.fries_condition = "mostly_saved".
- Set flag.demo.fry_defense_success = true.
- The bird drops a glossy feather while escaping.

PARTIAL — "A contested lunch"

- The bag tears; Lulu keeps a small portion while the bird takes the rest.
- Replace item.charles_fries with item.cold_fry_portion x1.
- Set variable.demo.fries_condition = "partial".
- Set flag.demo.fry_defense_partial = true.
- The bird drops a glossy feather.

FAIL — "Professionally robbed"

- The bird steals the entire bag, preserving the original demo's central beat.
- Remove item.charles_fries.
- Set variable.demo.fries_condition = "stolen".
- Set flag.demo.bird_stole_fries = true.
- The bird drops a glossy feather only after Lulu follows it to the next event.

Convergence rule
----------------

All outcomes advance to investigate_the_bird.
The objective changes according to wording, but the destination is the same.

Suggested objective text
------------------------

- Success: "Find out where that bird went."
- Partial: "Follow the bird—and what remains of lunch."
- Fail: "Find the bird that stole the fries."

Why success still continues the plot
------------------------------------

The goal is not to punish success by pretending it failed. Lulu protects most
of the food, gains a useful battle consumable, and pursues the bird because it
deliberately targeted her and dropped something strange. The original conflict
continues, but success remains materially better.

ACT 5 — THE BIRD'S DAYTIME HIDEOUT
==================================

Purpose
-------

Resolve the daytime chase, recover what can be recovered, establish the clue,
and guide the player naturally back toward Home.

Location
--------

Use the accessible grassy area south of Charles Jr., entered through the hedge
gap. The current Overworld npc_spawn near tile (11,24) is a suitable real anchor.
Do not place the objective inside the closed dirt-lot fence.

Stage A07 — Investigate the bird
--------------------------------

ID: investigate_the_bird
Target: Overworld / day_bird_spawn at or near current npc_spawn (11,24)

Approach behavior
-----------------

- The bird appears with branch-appropriate fry evidence.
- It does not speak in plain human language during daytime.
- It understands Lulu perfectly well and weaponizes eye contact.

Suggested event beats
---------------------

1. Lulu approaches.
2. The bird backs toward the hedge, guarding the bag/one stolen fry.
3. Lulu has a short choice:
   - "Give those back."
   - "What is your problem?"
   - "I walked all the way over here."
4. Choice changes only the exchange, not the branch outcome.
5. A second bird chirps from off-screen.
6. The fry thief immediately changes posture, drops a glossy black feather, and
   retreats.

Branch resolution
-----------------

SUCCESS:

- Lulu already has her surviving portion.
- The bird leaves behind the feather and one tiny paper band from the fry bag,
  arranged like a warning.

PARTIAL:

- Lulu retrieves the torn outer bag but not the missing food.
- Her cold fry portion remains usable.
- The feather is tucked deliberately under the bag.

FAIL:

- Lulu recovers the stolen bag, but it has been dragged through a parking lot and
  is no longer food.
- Do not return item.charles_fries as an edible item.
- This still fulfills the original "retrieve the fries" beat emotionally and
  visibly without asking the player to eat parking-lot fries.
- The glossy feather lies beneath the bag.

Shared result
-------------

- Give item.glossy_black_feather x1.
- Set flag.demo.day_bird_event_resolved = true.
- Set flag.demo.night_bird_thread_unlocked = true.
- Despawn the daytime fry thief.
- Objective: "Go home."

Clue item concept
-----------------

ID: item.glossy_black_feather
Category: key / weird
Description: "Too clean for something found under a stolen bag of fries. It
looks ordinary until the light moves."

Subtle night setup
------------------

On the walk home, the feather occasionally catches a color not otherwise used
in the daytime scene. This should be a small real-time tint/notification effect,
not a major hallucination sequence.

Stage A09 — Decide how to end the day
-------------------------------------

ID: choose_day_end
Objective: Decide what to do tonight.
Target: Home / bed_interaction

Before the choice, Lulu places the glossy feather somewhere visible near the
bed. The feather gives one quiet tap against the surface by itself.

Bed choices
-----------

1. Stay Up Tonight
2. Sleep Until Tomorrow
3. Cancel

Stay Up Tonight result
----------------------

- Complete Quest A.
- Set calendar.phase = night without changing calendar.day.
- Set flag.demo.stayed_up_for_birds = true.
- Start Quest B immediately at leave_home_at_night.
- Run a short night transition.

Sleep Until Tomorrow result
---------------------------

- Complete Quest A.
- Set flag.demo.slept_after_bird_day = true.
- Advance to Day 2 daytime normally.
- Recover Lulu and reduce insanity according to the central sleep rule.
- Quest B stays unlocked/pending.
- The next bed interaction on any later day can still offer Stay Up Tonight.
- The journal may show: "The feather can wait. If Lulu wants answers, she can
  stay up another night."

This is not a bad ending and does not permanently miss demo content.

Cancel result
-------------

- Close the bed menu.
- No state change.
- Keep the bed as the active objective until Quest A completes through one of
  the two real choices.

Optional soft demo checkpoint after normal sleep
------------------------------------------------

If the build needs a short-session stopping point, show "DAY 1 COMPLETE" after
waking. Do not show "END OF DEMO" while the night quest remains unfinished.

ACT 7 — NIGHT: THE NEIGHBORHOOD REGULAR
=======================================

Purpose
-------

Introduce night as a different layer of the same neighborhood, explain only
enough to motivate the next steps, and establish a recurring guide character.

Quest B start conditions
------------------------

- flag.demo.night_bird_thread_unlocked = true
- calendar.phase = night
- Quest B not completed

Stage B01 — Go outside
----------------------

ID: leave_home_at_night
Objective: Check outside.
Target: Home / entrance_exit

Night transition presentation
-----------------------------

- Use the current night tint.
- Change ambient NPC/event availability through conditions.
- Do not darken the map so much that mobile navigation becomes unclear.
- The bird feather gives another small visual pulse.

Night guide character concept
-----------------------------

Working role ID: npc.neighborhood_regular
Night variant role: The Night Guide
Final personal name: intentionally left for the user's writing pass

Day presentation:

- An ordinary neighborhood regular who may sometimes sit near the apartments.
- If encountered during a later daytime expansion, he is dry, observant, and not
  interested in explaining himself.

Night presentation:

- Same person, same clothes and real sprite, completely different authority.
- He treats bird politics as municipal infrastructure.
- He may call himself an "independent consultant" to the Department of Avian
  Affairs, though no such department is confirmed to exist.
- He is helpful, but every answer creates one smaller question.

Location
--------

Spawn on an accessible tile just outside Lulu's apartment, offset from the
player spawn and transition. Suggested target area: around tile (31,20), subject
to final collision/Combined View confirmation.

Stage B02 — Talk to the night guide
-----------------------------------

ID: meet_night_guide
Objective: Talk to the man outside.
Target: Overworld / night_guide_spawn

Suggested dialogue beats
------------------------

  GUIDE: You brought paperwork.
  LULU: It's a feather.
  GUIDE: That's what their paperwork looks like.

  GUIDE: You took food back from a bird on Division.
  LULU: It took food from me.
  GUIDE: That distinction is why they sent three.

Choice set
----------

- "Three what?"
- "How do you know about the fries?"
- "Why are we calling a feather paperwork?"

Every choice gets a distinct answer, then converges.

Guide information
-----------------

- The glossy feather is a challenge marker, not a random shed feather.
- The fry thief belongs to a small local gang.
- At night Lulu can notice things she filtered out during the day.
- The birds are gathering in the eastern parking area.
- Lulu should not confront them empty-handed.

Perception introduction
-----------------------

The guide asks Lulu to look at the feather again. At night it carries a faint
mark or alignment only Lulu can now perceive. This is a story introduction to
perception, not a full mechanics tutorial or an insanity increase.

Shared result
-------------

- Set flag.demo.met_night_guide = true.
- Set flag.demo.bird_gang_location_known = true.
- Unlock the bush weapon interaction.
- Objective: "Find something to defend yourself."

ACT 9 — THE BIRD GANG
======================

Purpose
-------

Deliver the first surreal-night payoff and teach the turn-based battle loop.

Location
--------

Use the broad accessible eastern parking area. The current Overworld npc_spawn
near tile (42,24) is a suitable real anchor and keeps the encounter away from
doors and narrow paths.

Stage B04 — Reach the gang
--------------------------

ID: confront_bird_gang
Objective: Confront the bird gang.
Target: Overworld / bird_gang_spawn near current npc_spawn (42,24)

Approach cutscene
-----------------

1. Lock movement at a safe distance.
2. Spawn three birds in a readable formation.
3. The daytime fry thief stands behind or among them.
4. The glossy feather reacts.
5. The guide remains outside the formation; he does not fight for Lulu.
6. Short confrontation dialogue.

Suggested confrontation
------------------------

  LULU: Are these the three?
  GUIDE: Numerically, yes.

  [The center bird pecks once at the pavement. The others spread out.]

  LULU: I want it noted that they started this.
  GUIDE: Noted by whom?
  LULU: Me.

Enemy formation concept
-----------------------

Battle ID: battle.demo.bird_gang

1. Bird Lookout
   - Lower HP.
   - Uses Warning Cry to reduce Lulu's next attack or raise an ally's defense.

2. Fry Thief
   - The daytime recurring bird.
   - Fastest enemy.
   - Uses Peck and Bag Feint.

3. Peck Captain
   - Highest HP.
   - Uses Peck and Wing Guard.
   - Defeating the captain ends the birds' willingness to continue once all
     active enemies are down.

All three may reuse the existing real bird sprite with position/name/behavior
distinction. Do not invent substitute enemy art.

Tutorial command set
--------------------

- Attack
- Guard
- Item
- Stand Your Ground

Stand Your Ground concept
-------------------------

- Lulu's first simple skill.
- Reduces incoming damage for the round and prevents one attack-reduction effect.
- No elaborate magic animation is required.
- It establishes that Lulu's combat identity includes composure, not only sword
  attacks.

Branch carryover into battle
----------------------------

SUCCESS at fry defense:

- item.emergency_fry_portion is available once.
- Suggested effect: restore a small amount of HP.
- Battle intro line acknowledges Lulu saved most of the bag.

PARTIAL:

- item.cold_fry_portion is available once with a smaller heal.
- Battle intro line acknowledges the torn bag.

FAIL:

- No fry consumable.
- The battle remains balanced and winnable without it.
- Guide may give a one-time pre-battle full heal, but not a substitute item.

Battle teaching sequence
------------------------

- First command screen briefly highlights Attack.
- After the first enemy action, briefly explain Guard/Stand Your Ground.
- Explain Item only if the player has a surviving fry item.
- Do not interrupt every turn.
- Touch targets remain large and labels remain visible in landscape.

Victory result
--------------

- Set flag.demo.bird_gang_defeated = true.
- Set flag.demo.first_turn_battle_won = true.
- Remove the challenge feather from active quest use, or transform it into a
  harmless keepsake item.bird_gang_feather.
- Birds are defeated, not killed. They retreat to remain recurring characters.
- The fry thief gives Lulu one final deeply resentful look.

Suggested victory beat
----------------------

  [The Peck Captain drops the glossy feather at Lulu's feet.]
  GUIDE: That means the matter is settled.
  LULU: Good.
  GUIDE: Locally.
  LULU: I liked the first sentence better.

Reward concept
--------------

- Keep the Bush Sword.
- Unlock Stand Your Ground permanently.
- Keep item.bird_gang_feather as a zero-weight key/record item if the inventory
  design supports keepsakes.
- No money/XP economy is required for this demo.

Defeat/retry result
-------------------

- Do not advance the quest.
- Restore the pre-battle checkpoint at the edge of the encounter.
- Restore consumables to their pre-battle quantities.
- The guide gives a short branch-safe line:

  GUIDE: They remain three.
  LULU: I noticed.

- Offer Retry / Step Away.
- Step Away returns to world mode with the objective unchanged.
- Sleeping remains available only if the event runner is not inside battle, but
  the night quest remains active for a later attempt.

ID: return_home_after_battle

Optional guide farewell

  GUIDE: They live here.
  LULU: Right.
  GUIDE: But next time they will announce themselves.

Stage B06 — Sleep until morning
-------------------------------

ID: sleep_after_first_night
Objective: Get some sleep.
Target sequence:

1. Overworld / transition_to_home
2. Home / bed_interaction

Night bed menu
--------------

- Sleep Until Morning
- Cancel

Sleep result
------------

- Complete Quest B.
- Advance calendar.day by 1.
- Set calendar.phase = day.
- Apply normal sleep recovery and insanity reduction.
- Autosave.
- Wake Lulu at the current safe Home wake position.
- Set flag.demo.complete = true.

Final scene
-----------

The dog is awake before Lulu. Near the window is a tiny strip of paper marked
with three precise bird footprints and one grease spot.

Suggested final exchange:

  LULU: Is that a receipt?
  DOG: [looks away]

Final card
----------

DAY 2

THE BIRDS WILL REMEMBER THIS.

END OF DEMO

Do not imply the whole game ends; this is a clean vertical-slice endpoint.

BRANCH CONSISTENCY MATRIX
=========================

Fry defense success
-------------------

- Plot continues because the bird took one fry and dropped/led to the clue.
- Lulu keeps the best optional battle consumable.
- Bird hideout dialogue recognizes Lulu won the struggle.
- Night guide dialogue recognizes the unusually tidy feather challenge.
- Bird battle remains mandatory only when the player chooses a night phase.

Fry defense partial
-------------------

- Plot continues through the torn bag and clue.
- Lulu keeps a smaller consumable.
- Dialogue recognizes the split outcome.
- Same night quest stages and final reward.

Fry defense fail
----------------

- Preserves the original full theft/chase.
- Lulu retrieves the bag, but not edible fries.
- No fry consumable in battle.
- Battle is still balanced and fully winnable.
- Same night quest stages and final reward.

Sleep after Day 1
-----------------

- Quest A completes.
- Day advances normally.
- Quest B remains pending, not failed.
- On a later night, B01 begins when Lulu chooses Stay Up Tonight.
- No special content is permanently lost.

Stay up after Day 1
-------------------

- Quest A completes.
- Day remains 1.
- Night phase starts.
- Quest B activates immediately.

Battle defeat
-------------

- Quest remains on B04.
- Pre-battle state/consumables restore.
- Retry or step away.
- No duplicate rewards or repeated quest advancement.

Battle victory
--------------

- Quest advances exactly once.
- Encounter deactivates.
- Return-home and sleep ending become available.

WRITING GUIDELINES FOR THE NEXT REVIEW
======================================

Lulu
----

- Dry, practical, and willing to argue with absurdity.
- She notices strange details but does not instantly accept magical explanations.
- Keep most lines short enough for a phone dialogue box.
- Her comedy should not depend on cruelty or ignorance.

Dog
---

- Nonverbal during this demo.
- Reactions create timing and personality.
- Do not reveal animal speech before the perception system deliberately earns it.

Cashier
-------

- A normal person doing a normal shift.
- Provides contrast with the later night material.
- Do not make every NPC secretly aware of the supernatural.

Night guide
-----------

- Speaks with calm bureaucratic certainty about impossible things.
- Never dumps full lore.
- Answers one question and reframes another.
- Helpful without solving the conflict for Lulu.
- Final name, deeper history, and daytime role should be chosen in the user's
  later fine-tuning pass.

Birds
-----

- Organized enough to be unsettling, petty enough to stay funny.
- Day birds communicate through behavior and sound.
- Night perception may make intent clearer without immediately giving every bird
  full spoken dialogue.
- They retreat after battle; they are recurring neighborhood rivals.

Dialogue length
---------------

- Routine interactions: 1-3 short pages.
- Important conversation: 4-8 short pages plus choices.
- Pre-battle exchange: under 30 seconds on a normal read.
- Never place a long monologue between the player and a newly updated objective.

IMPLEMENTATION ACCEPTANCE CHECKLIST
===================================

Quest system
------------

- Both quests are definitions, not main.ts stage switches.
- Every stage, objective, listener, and transition has a stable ID.
- All content references pass validation.
- One active objective marker appears at a time.
- Every branch has an automated event-driven test.

Day flow
--------

- Fridge gives dog food.
- Feeding dog consumes it.
- Charles order gives fries.
- Bird ambush fires once and not on transition frame.
- Minigame returns all three results.
- Bird hideout is reachable.
- Each result converges without contradictory inventory.
- Glossy feather unlocks the night thread.

Bed/day-night
-------------

- No timer changes phase.
- Stay Up enters night without incrementing day.
- Sleep advances the day and keeps Quest B available later.
- Cancel changes nothing.
- Night sleep completes the demo and advances to morning.

Night flow
----------

- Night guide appears only under valid conditions.
- Bush sword uses a real existing bush interaction and real project weapon art.
- Sword enters inventory/equipment.
- Bird gang uses the open eastern parking area.
- Battle is turn-based.
- Defeat safely retries.
- Victory advances once and deactivates the encounter.

Save/load
---------

- Save/load tested at every A and B stage boundary.
- Minigame result persists after convergence.
- Sleep/stay-up choice persists.
- Pre-battle checkpoint cannot duplicate items/rewards.
- Completed demo remains completed after reload.

Mobile presentation
-------------------

- Dialogue choices and battle commands have large touch targets.
- Movement input cannot leak into menus/minigame/battle.
- Objective and marker remain readable in landscape safe areas.
- Night tint does not obscure paths, doors, or foreground depth.
- User performs final phone playtest.
